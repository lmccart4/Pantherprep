PSAT-Specific

Understanding the Adaptive Format

The PSAT Math section has 44 questions across two 35-minute modules. Here's the critical strategic insight:

Module 1 ‚Üí determines Module 2 difficulty. If you score ~60‚Äì70%+ on Module 1, you get the harder Module 2 ‚Äî which unlocks the highest score range (640‚Äì760). If you get the easier Module 2, your score is capped around 620‚Äì640 regardless of how well you do.

Strategic implication: Module 1 accuracy is MORE important than Module 2 accuracy. Take your time in Module 1 ‚Äî getting routed to the harder Module 2 is how you reach National Merit territory.

National Merit Math Target: To reach a Selection Index of ~218+ (typical Semifinalist cutoff), you need a Math score of roughly 700+. That requires routing to the hard Module 2 AND scoring well on it.

Strategy 1

Plug In Your Own Numbers (PIYON)

When to use: Variables in answer choices. "Which expression is equivalent to‚Ä¶" Questions with no specific values.
How It Works ‚Äî Step by Step
‚ñ∏

Step 1: Choose simple numbers. Best picks: 2, 3, 5, 10, or 100 (for percents).

Step 2: Work the problem with your numbers to get a TARGET answer.

Step 3: Plug the SAME numbers into each answer choice.

Step 4: The choice that matches your target is correct.

Avoid 0 and 1 ‚Äî they have special properties that make multiple answers look correct. If two answers match, try different numbers.

Worked Example

A) x + 2   B) x¬≤ + 2   C) 2x   D) x
Plug In x = 3: (9 + 6) / 3 = 15/3 = 5 ‚Üê target
A) 3 + 2 = 5 ‚úì   B) 11 ‚úó   C) 6 ‚úó   D) 3 ‚úó
Answer: A
Strategy 2

Backsolve (Plug In The Answers / PITA)

When to use: Answer choices are numbers. "What is the value of‚Ä¶" Systems where algebra feels messy.
How It Works ‚Äî Step by Step
‚ñ∏

Step 1: Start with choice B or C (the middle value).

Step 2: Plug that value into the problem and check if it works.

Step 3: If wrong, determine if you need larger or smaller ‚Üí eliminates 2‚Äì3 choices at once!

Backsolve is powerful when algebra would be complex but testing a number is simple.

Worked Example
If 3x + 7 = 2x + 12, what is the value of x?
A) 3   B) 5   C) 7   D) 12
Try B) x = 5: 3(5) + 7 = 22. 2(5) + 12 = 22. ‚úì Equal!
Answer: B ‚Äî found on first try.
Strategy 3

Ballpark & POE (Process of Elimination)

When to use: ANY question where estimation eliminates choices. Geometry with figures. Late-module questions when time is short.
How It Works
‚ñ∏

Before calculating: "Should this be big or small? Positive or negative? Close to what?"

18% of 412 ‚âà 20% of 400 = 80. Eliminate anything far from 80.

Triangle with sides 5 and 12? Hypotenuse is between 12 and 17. Eliminate 8 and 25.

POE shortcuts: Question asks for positive ‚Üí eliminate negatives. Context requires small ‚Üí eliminate large.

Common Traps

The Top 8 Preventable Mistakes

These mistakes have nothing to do with math ability ‚Äî they‚Äôre about careful reading and attention. Fixing them alone can be worth 3‚Äì5 questions per test.

Trap 1: Solving for the Wrong Variable
The question asks for 6x ‚àí 14, not x. You solve for x = 9 and pick 9. The answer was 40.
‚úì Fix: Underline exactly what the question asks for BEFORE solving.
Trap 2: Intermediate vs. Final Answer
‚ÄúA shirt is 20% off, then taxed 8%. What is the total cost?‚Äù You find the discount ($32) and pick that ‚Äî but the question asks for the final price after tax ($34.56).
‚úì Fix: Make sure your answer addresses the FINAL part of the question.
Trap 3: ‚ÄúAt Least‚Äù vs. ‚ÄúMore Than‚Äù
‚ÄúAt least 5‚Äù means ‚â• 5 (includes 5). ‚ÄúMore than 5‚Äù means > 5 (does NOT include 5). Mixing these up changes the answer.
‚úì Fix: ‚ÄúAt least‚Äù = ‚â•. ‚ÄúMore than‚Äù = >. ‚ÄúNo more than‚Äù = ‚â§. ‚ÄúFewer than‚Äù = <.
Trap 4: Negation Words
‚ÄúWhich is NOT a solution?‚Äù or ‚ÄúWhich CANNOT be true?‚Äù Your brain skips the NOT and you pick a valid answer. Wrong.
‚úì Fix: Circle negative words (NOT, CANNOT, EXCEPT, LEAST) when you see them.

A graph‚Äôs y-axis is labeled ‚Äúin thousands.‚Äù You read a point as y = 45 instead of y = 45,000. Or x-axis is months but the question asks about years.
‚úì Fix: Always read axis labels AND the scale before reading any data.

‚úì Fix: For grid-ins, double-check the sign. For ‚Äúincrease/decrease‚Äù questions, give positive values.
Trap 7: Wrong Denominator for Percent Change

‚úì Fix: % change = (new ‚àí old) / OLD √ó 100. Denominator = starting value.

‚úì Fix: ‚ÄúValue of x‚Äù means the input. ‚ÄúValue of f(x)‚Äù means the output. Read which one.

Summary

Key Takeaways

  1. Use the 5-Second Decision Tree on EVERY question: direct ‚Üí Plug In ‚Üí Backsolve ‚Üí Desmos ‚Üí Ballpark/POE.
  2. Variables in choices = Plug In. Number choices = Backsolve. Functions/graphs = Desmos.
  3. Ballpark BEFORE calculating ‚Äî eliminate 1‚Äì2 obviously wrong choices on almost any question.
  4. Module 1 accuracy matters most ‚Äî getting routed to hard Module 2 unlocks the highest scores.
  5. Time budget: First pass ~25 min, Review ~8 min, LOTD ~2 min. Check pace at Q11.
  6. Grid-ins: Always re-read what the question asks for. The #1 trap is answering x when they asked for 2x.
  7. LOTD (Letter of the Day): Pick one letter (e.g., B) for ALL remaining guesses. Never leave blanks.
