The Discriminant: How Many Solutions?

The expression under the square root ‚Äî b¬≤ ‚àí 4ac ‚Äî is called the discriminant. It tells you how many real solutions:

Function Notation & Transformations
‚ñ∏

f(x) is NOT "f times x". It means "the output of function f when the input is x."

f(3) = plug in x = 3 and evaluate.

Rational Expressions & Equations

Simplifying Rational Expressions
‚ñ∏

A rational expression is a fraction with polynomials. To simplify:

  • 1. Factor both numerator and denominator completely.
  • 2. Cancel any common factors.

Example: (x¬≤ ‚àí 9) / (x¬≤ + 5x + 6) = (x+3)(x‚àí3) / (x+2)(x+3) = (x‚àí3) / (x+2)

Domain restrictions: Any value making the denominator zero is excluded. Above: x ‚â† ‚àí2 and x ‚â† ‚àí3.

Solving Rational Equations
‚ñ∏
  • 1. Find the LCD of all fractions.
  • 2. Multiply every term by the LCD to clear fractions.
  • 3. Solve the resulting equation.
  • 4. CHECK for extraneous solutions ‚Äî plug back in to make sure no denominator = 0.

Example: 1/x + 1/3 = 1/2 ‚Üí LCD = 6x ‚Üí 6 + 2x = 3x ‚Üí x = 6 ‚úì

PSAT Trap: Rational equation questions almost always include an extraneous solution ‚Äî an answer that makes a denominator zero. Always check your work.
