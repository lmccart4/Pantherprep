Module 5

Geometry & Trigonometry

Fewer questions (~15%), but highly formulaic — memorize the key facts and these become free points.

⏱ ~60 min📋 4 topics🔨 Phase 2: Build
The good news: Geometry & Trig questions are the most formula-dependent on the PSAT. If you memorize the formulas and recognize the patterns, these 5–7 questions become nearly free points.
Topic 5A

Lines, Angles & Triangles

Angle Relationships
▸
  • Vertical angles are equal (formed by intersecting lines, across from each other)
  • Supplementary angles add to 180° (on a straight line)
  • Complementary angles add to 90° (form a right angle)

Parallel Lines + Transversal

  • Corresponding angles: EQUAL (same position at each intersection)
  • Alternate interior angles: EQUAL (opposite sides, between the parallels)
  • Co-interior (same-side interior): SUPPLEMENTARY (add to 180°)
Triangle Facts
▸
  • Angle sum: Interior angles always add to 180°
  • Exterior angle theorem: Exterior angle = sum of two remote interior angles
  • Similar triangles: Same shape, proportional sides. Set up a proportion to find missing sides.
  • Triangle inequality: Any side < sum of the other two sides
Special Right Triangles & Pythagorean Theorem
▸

Pythagorean Theorem: a² + b² = c² (c = hypotenuse, always the longest side)

These special triangles appear on nearly every PSAT. Memorize the ratios cold.

30-60-90
1 : √3 : 2
Short leg : Long leg : Hypotenuse
Opp 30° : Opp 60° : Opp 90°
45-45-90
1 : 1 : √2
Leg : Leg : Hypotenuse
Two legs are always equal

Common Pythagorean Triples (memorize these!):

3 – 4 – 5
5 – 12 – 13
8 – 15 – 17
7 – 24 – 25
6 – 8 – 10
9 – 12 – 15

Last two are multiples of 3-4-5. Any multiple of a triple is also a triple!

Topic 5B

Circles

Standard Form
(x−h)²+(y−k)²=r²
Center
(h, k)
Area
A = πr²
Circumference
C = 2πr
Arc Length
(θ/360) × 2πr
Sector Area
(θ/360) × πr²
Converting General Form to Standard Form
▸

Sometimes the PSAT gives: x² + y² + Dx + Ey + F = 0

Strategy: Complete the square for both x and y.

Example: x² + y² − 6x + 4y − 12 = 0

Group: (x² − 6x) + (y² + 4y) = 12

Complete: (x² − 6x + 9) + (y² + 4y + 4) = 12 + 9 + 4

Result: (x − 3)² + (y + 2)² = 25 → Center (3, −2), Radius 5

Tangent Lines & Inscribed Angles
▸
  • Tangent line: Touches circle at one point. Perpendicular to radius (90°).
  • Central angle: Vertex at center. Equal to intercepted arc.
  • Inscribed angle: Vertex on circle. Equals HALF the intercepted arc.
Desmos: Type (x−2)²+(y+3)²=25 directly to graph any circle. Find intersections with lines by graphing both.

Topic 5C

Area, Perimeter & Volume

These formulas are on the reference sheet, but memorizing them saves valuable time.

A = lw

V = ⅓πr²h
Sphere
V = ⁴⁄₃πr³

Topic 5D

Trigonometry

SOH-CAH-TOA
Sin = Opp / Hyp
Cos = Adj / Hyp
Tan = Opp / Adj
Key Relationships
sin(x) = cos(90° − x)
tan(x) = sin(x) / cos(x)
sin²(x) + cos²(x) = 1
180° = π radians
Radians ↔ Degrees
▸

Degrees → Radians: multiply by π/180

Radians → Degrees: multiply by 180/π

Common conversions: 30° = π/6 · 45° = π/4 · 60° = π/3 · 90° = π/2 · 180° = π · 360° = 2π

Desmos: Defaults to radians. Click the wrench icon ⚙ to switch to degrees if needed.
Complementary Angle Relationship
▸

sin(x) = cos(90° − x) and cos(x) = sin(90° − x)

In a right triangle, the two acute angles add to 90°. The side opposite one angle is adjacent to the other.

PSAT question type: "If sin(x) = cos(32°), what is x?" → x = 90° − 32° = 58°.

Coordinate Geometry
▸

Distance formula: d = √((x₂−x₁)² + (y₂−y₁)²) — just Pythagorean theorem on a coordinate plane

Midpoint formula: M = ((x₁+x₂)/2, (y₁+y₂)/2) — average the coordinates

These often appear combined with circle or line questions on the PSAT.

Math Trap Taxonomy

Four Traps That Recur in Geometry & Trig

Learn these by name. When you spot one in a problem, you'll know exactly what to watch for.

1
🎯 The Wrong Target
Solved for the wrong measurement — found diameter when they asked for radius, found area when they asked for circumference.
Diameter = 10, Area = π(5²) = 25π (not π(10²) = 100π)
2
📐 The Phantom Height
Used the slant height instead of the actual height in a volume or area formula. The height must be perpendicular to the base.
Cone: V = ⅓πr²h uses vertical height, NOT slant height
3
🔄 The Sign Flip
In circle equations, (x+3)² means h = −3, not h = 3. The sign inside the parentheses is opposite to the center coordinate.
(x+1)²+(y−4)²=9 → Center (−1, 4), NOT (1, −4)
4
📏 The Unit Mismatch
Mixed up degrees and radians, or used inches for one dimension and feet for another.
sin(30) in Desmos = sin(30 radians) ≠ sin(30°). Switch to degree mode!
